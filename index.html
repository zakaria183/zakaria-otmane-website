About Me

I am Zakaria Otmane, a fifth-year applied mathematics student at INSA Rennes in France. After completing two years of preparatory classes (MPSI–MP) at Lycée Dupuy de Lôme, I joined INSA Rennes, where I have been actively involved in mathematical research projects. My main areas of interest include partial differential equations, probability theory, and optimization.

To strengthen my theoretical background, I am pursuing a double degree including a Master’s in Fundamental Mathematics at Université de Rennes. This path reflects my ambition to deepen my understanding of modern mathematical analysis and prepare for advanced research.

Projects

  • Project 1: Mathematical modeling of crowd evacuation

    This project was carried out in the second year of the MP class as part of the TIPE (Supervised Personal Initiative Project in English). The objective was to assess the relevance of a macroscopic model in predicting and analyzing an emergency evacuation of a small park, considering safety constraints. To be more precise, I was tasked with studying the movement of a crowd as if it were the flow of a fluid, applying the macroscopic laws of fluid mechanics. This study involved, among other things, the theory of Eulerian descriptions, particle derivatives, and the method of characteristics to solve partial differential equations, such as the mass conservation equation.

  • Project 2: Complex Analysis and Study of the Riemann Zeta Function

    This project, undertaken as part of my fourth year of studies at INSA, involved writing a thesis in which I conducted an in-depth study of the Riemann zeta function. This function is a central object in complex analysis and number theory. My goal was to explore its analytic and arithmetic properties, including its analytic continuation, functional equation, and its profound connections with prime numbers. The thesis also includes a numerical study of the representations of this function using Python.

  • Project 3: Holomorphic ODEs Around the Notion of Monodromy and Regular Singularities

    As part of a research collaboration with IRMAR during my fourth year at INSA, I conducted a thesis exploring holomorphic differential equations and the concept of monodromy. These equations are key objects in complex analysis, particularly when studying the behavior of solutions near singularities. My aim was to understand how solutions transform under analytic continuation around such points, using tools from algebraic topology. The thesis also includes a theoretical analysis of systems with regular singularities.

  • Project 4: Introduction to Optimal Transport – Theory and Numerics

    This project, conducted as part of my fourth-year internship with INSA Rennes, is carried out in collaboration with the Grupo de Física Matemática at Instituto Superior Técnico, Lisbon. The goal was to explore the theoretical foundations and numerical methods of optimal transport, a rich mathematical field at the intersection of analysis, variational calculus, and probability, with significant impact in the past two decades.

    After studying core concepts such as the Monge and Kantorovich formulations, the dual problem, and entropic regularization, I implemented efficient numerical algorithms in Python. The goal was to produce realistic animations by using the Wasserstein distance in optimal transport applications, including image morphing and inpainting.

    This project is carried out as part of my Master’s 1 in Fundamental Mathematics.
